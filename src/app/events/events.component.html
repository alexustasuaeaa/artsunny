18.04.2025 KC Radionica, Belgrade, Serbia.
You know, man, the soul isn’t tied to the body — it’s tied to the place a person calls home. That’s why an American working in Beijing eats baozi for breakfast while reading about debates in Congress. A Russian in Hanoi, sipping rou run in the evening, shakes his head at the latest laws passed by the Duma. And that’s why a Serb who’s spent twenty years in America types “JBT” in a message after reading news from Belgrade and washes it down with bourbon instead of rakia.

When someone loses their country, their soul lingers in the airport, lost in the crowd of goodbyes, before boarding a flight with no real destination. They try to settle in, to make a home elsewhere, but there’s always that emptiness — where the soul used to be. Some ignore it. Some drown it in love or liquor. Others try to capture it, in words or on canvas.

This exhibition explores that void, an attempt to capture the state of “nowhere” — a space where the soul, having lost its footing, searches for itself in lines, colors, and shapes. There are no clear boundaries here — just as there are none between home and exile, memory and oblivion. Each visitor arrives with their own “nowhere,” and perhaps, in this emptiness, something new is born: a dialogue, an understanding, or simply the quiet contemplation of something that has always been inside, yet remained unspoken.
